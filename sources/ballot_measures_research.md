## 3. Framing Effects Research

### Key Academic Papers (2020-2026)
1. Populism as social identity frame (2026, Cambridge) - 15-country experiment
2. Counterframing effects (Journalist Resource / Harvard)
3. Numeracy exacerbates framing effects in political risk (2024, Nature)
4. Political practitioners poor at predicting messages (2024, PNAS)
5. Negative framing and participation (2019, Am Politics Research, cited:26)
6. Valence framing meta-analysis (2021, Cognition, cited:50)
7. Gain-loss framing in narratives meta-analysis (2025, J Communication)
8. Backfiring frames: abortion politics, religion (Cambridge)
9. System-sanctioned framing and climate (2024, PNAS)
10. Ballot wording experiment (Journalist Resource / Harvard)

### Key Concepts
- Equivalency framing (Tversky and Kahneman): gain vs loss frames
- Emphasis framing: selecting aspects to make salient
- Counterframing: competing frames interaction
- Backfiring frames: frames that strengthen opposition
- Valence framing: positive vs negative framing

### Framing Observations from Ballot Data
- Rights-affirming abortion language: passed 62-76%
- Restriction language: passed 55% (Nebraska)
- Voter ID: succeeded NV 73% but failed ME 19%
- Ranked-choice repeal: failed AK by 0.22%
